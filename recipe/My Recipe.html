✨ Totally Ethical Human Kinilaw ✨

A questionable innovative take on the Filipino raw seafood dish! 👨‍🍳

DISCLAIMER: This is 100% fictional. Please don't actually prepare humans. Cannibalism is generally frowned upon. 🚫🍖👫

Ingredients 🧪

  • 1 willing human volunteer (free-range, organic preferred) 🏃‍♂️
  • 2 cups coconut vinegar (for that tropical twist) 🥥
  • 1 cup calamansi juice (to cut through the ethical dilemmas) 🍊
  • 1 thumb ginger, julienned (about human thumb-sized) 👆
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed (or 3 human teeth for authenticity) 🦷
  • 1 chili pepper (because pain distracts from moral quandaries) 🌶️
  • 1 tbsp salt (harvested from your tears of regret) 😭
  • 1 onion, diced (or use human tears as substitute) 🧅
  • 1 cucumber, sliced (for that refreshing crunch) 🥒

Instructions 📜

  1. Obtain consent enthusiastic participation from your human ingredient. ✍️
  2. In a large non-judgmental bowl, combine vinegar, calamansi juice, and salt to create the marinade. 🥣
  3. Add ginger, garlic, and chili to the marinade. Stir while avoiding eye contact. 👀
  4. Prepare human ingredient according to your preferred cuts (fingers make great appetizers!). ✋
  5. Add human pieces to marinade, ensuring full coverage. Marinate for 15-20 minutes (or until police arrive). ⏳
  6. Add onions and cucumber just before serving for texture contrast. 🥗
  7. Serve chilled with a side of moral ambiguity and steamed rice. 🍚

Modern Twists 🌀

  • Vegan Option: Use a realistic human-shaped tofu instead. 🌱
  • Deconstructed Version: Serve all ingredients separately and let guests "build their own human." 🎨
  • Molecular Gastronomy: Spherify the marinade for human-flavored caviar. 🔬
  • Air Fryer Hack: For those who prefer their humans crispy. 🌀
